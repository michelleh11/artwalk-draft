Description: Rene Magritte inspired ceramic students to sculpt in clay and create two surreal lamp posts.

Extended Description and Concept: Students went on a field trip to the San Francisco Museum of Modern Art to see the Rene Magritte's exhibition The Fifth Season. Students studied Magritte's subject matter, concept, and painting style. They were encouraged to find elements that they liked with his work. They were to make work that was inspired by Rene Magritte which could be highly dimensional or have a smooth flat surface, depending on their idea. Technically the students were encouraged to take advantage of the clay and the glazes and embrace Magritte's juxtaposition in subject matter.

Developing My Flowers, Christine Siska
Transcript

  • After getting feedback on how I could make a branch, I decided to go for it and see what happened. I made a plan for assembling a main branch with the hole and smaller branches to attach the actual flowers. After assembling that using coils, I rolled a branch on the clay to give it natural texture. Then I wrapped it in plastic. I made petals for the dogwood flowers and set those aside in plastic. Then I went back and added the tiny inner beads using wet slip and scoring. When all the flowers were done, I scored and slipped the flowers to the branches as they dried out. I had to work around the piece as it dried at different rates and I had to balance getting the flowers on without being too heavy for the structure of the branch.

    I really like having the flowers on campus. I was a little surprised when I first saw it in the syllabus because I didn't think a beginner's class would be able to make art that was "good enough" for an art installation. I'm glad I was wrong - I learned a lot just by seeing what other people in our class made and I was really happy we got to challenge ourselves and each other with this project. Christine Siska
